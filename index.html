For Parents

Our approach, what we do
& how we train.

Too many programs put kids on a court, roll out a basket of shuttles, and call it training. Your child deserves more than supervised play.

At The Badminton Project, every session is structured, every drill has a purpose, and every young athlete is developed step by step — technique, footwork, athleticism, and match craft — taught by a coach who competed at the top of the sport.

Whether your child is picking up a racquet for the first time or already competing and stalling out somewhere else, we build them properly — and we can show you the difference.

Who We Are

Every young athlete
begins as a project.

The Badminton Project exists to give kids and teens genuine coaching and competitive development — the kind that’s planned, purposeful, and taken seriously every session.

We develop young athletes deliberately: beginners learning the game the right way from day one, developing players finding their competitive edge, and committed juniors chasing the top of the sport. Whatever the level, every child gets the same thing — a real coach, a real plan, and real standards.

Progress here is earned step by step. That’s not a limitation. That’s the promise — and it’s why our athletes actually improve.

What We Do

Training with intention.

Our Academy develops young athletes through four training streams — technique, competition, foundational skills, and athletic development — each planned with the same care, whether your child is chasing titles or finding their first competitive rally. Nothing here is improvised. Every stream is a deliberate step in a longer plan.
